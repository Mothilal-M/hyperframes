A FIELD GUIDE — IN TWO IDEAS

Reasoning,
and acting.

Strip away the diagrams and the acronyms — modern AI architecture rests on two simple verbs.
THE CHATBOT
Responds.
Generates an answer from text patterns alone — confident, but ungrounded.
THE AGENT
Reasons. Acts. Observes.
Decides, uses tools, reads what came back — and tries again until the work is done.
CHAPTER IIThe Mental Model
II
THE SIMPLEST MENTAL MODEL

Three roles. One loop.

i.
The Brain
Reasoning
Decides what should happen next, given the goal and what's known.
ii.
The Hands
Acting
Calls tools, queries data, writes code, sends a request to the world.
iii.
The Feedback
Observation
Reads the result that came back — then reasons all over again.
CHATBOT · GENERIC
"Improve marketing, optimize pricing, talk to customers." — sounds useful, but not based on your data.
AGENT · GROUNDED
Queries the database, fetches last week's report, compares trends, and writes three suggestions tied to actual numbers.
CHAPTER IIIThe Reasoning Layer
III
LAYER ONE

The reasoning layer.

Before doing anything, the AI asks itself a few quiet questions.
i.What is the task?
ii.What information do I already have?
iii.What information is missing?
iv.Can I answer directly, or do I need a tool?
v.Is this a safe action?

CHAPTER VThe Loop
V
THE AGENT LOOP

Think. Act. Observe. Answer.

i.Think
ii.Act
iii.Observe
iv.Think again
v.Answer

EXAMPLE — REVIEW MY LANDING PAGE

i.User gives a goal: review this landing page.
ii.AI plans: I'll need copy, analytics, and feedback.
iii.AI acts: reads the page, fetches analytics, searches notes.
iv.AI observes: bounce is high, headline unclear, CTA too low.
v.AI reasons: not just design — also a messaging issue.
vi.AI answers with three suggestions, grounded in the data.
